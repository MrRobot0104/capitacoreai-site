The Anatomy of a Great Prompt
1. Role
Define who the AI should be. This sets the context and expertise level. Example: "You are a marketing expert with 20 years of experience in B2B SaaS."
2. Context
Provide background information the AI needs to understand your situation. Give relevant facts, constraints, and current state. The more specific, the better.
3. Task
Clearly state what you want the AI to do. Use action verbs: create, analyze, summarize, brainstorm, evaluate. Be specific about deliverables.
4. Format
Specify how you want the output structured. Examples: bullet points, table, step-by-step guide, JSON, CSV. This saves time and improves usability.
5. Constraints
Set boundaries: tone, length, audience, style. Example: "Keep it under 100 words, use casual language, avoid jargon."
6 Proven Prompt Formulas
Formula 1: Role + Context + Task + Format
You are [ROLE]. [CONTEXT]. Please [TASK]. Format as [FORMAT].
Formula 2: Problem + Approach + Constraints
I have [PROBLEM]. I've tried [APPROACH]. Please [TASK] considering [CONSTRAINTS].
Formula 3: Goal + Current State + Desired State
Goal: [GOAL]. Currently: [STATE]. Desired: [STATE]. How do I [TASK]?
Formula 4: Framework + Analysis + Action
Using [FRAMEWORK], analyze [SUBJECT]. Then provide [DELIVERABLE].
Formula 5: Example-Based Learning
Here's an example: [EXAMPLE]. Now create something similar for [NEW_CONTEXT].
Formula 6: Step-by-Step Breakdown
Break down [TASK] into detailed steps. For each step: explain it, provide an example, suggest best practices.
8 Common Mistakes to Avoid
❌ Mistake 1: Being Too Vague
Bad: "Write some marketing copy." Good: "Write a 100-word email subject line and preview text for a SaaS product launch targeting finance directors."
❌ Mistake 2: Forgetting Context
Always explain your situation. The AI can't read your mind. Include: industry, target audience, current state, constraints, goals.
❌ Mistake 3: Unclear Output Format
Bad: "Give me ideas." Good: "Give me 5 ideas as a numbered list with brief 1-sentence descriptions for each."
❌ Mistake 4: No Role Definition
Assigning a role helps AI match the expertise and tone you need. Compare: generic answer vs. "Answer as a veteran product manager."
❌ Mistake 5: Skipping Success Criteria
Tell the AI what "good" looks like. Example: "Make it persuasive, data-backed, and suitable for C-level executives."
❌ Mistake 6: Asking Multiple Unrelated Questions
One prompt = one task. If you have 3 questions, ask 3 prompts. It improves focus and quality.
❌ Mistake 7: Not Iterating
AI outputs improve with feedback. Say: "Too formal, make it casual" or "Add more examples" or "Shorten this section."
❌ Mistake 8: Assuming One-Shot Perfection
Treat AI as a collaborative tool. Review the output, ask follow-up questions, request refinements until it's perfect.
Advanced Techniques
Chain-of-Thought Prompting
Ask AI to show its reasoning. Example: "Walk me through your thinking step-by-step before giving the final answer." This improves accuracy on complex tasks.
Few-Shot Learning
Provide examples of what you want. Example: "Here are 2 examples of the format I want. Now create 5 more like these..." Better results than description alone.
Role-Playing & Personas
Make AI adopt a specific persona. "You are a grumpy 60-year-old software engineer reviewing code" produces different output than generic roles.
Temperature Guidance
For creative tasks: "Be creative and take risks." For analytical: "Be precise and conservative." This guides AI behavior.
Constraint-Based Prompting
Set hard limits: "Use only these 5 words" or "Answer in exactly 50 words" or "Must include a data-backed statistic." Constraints drive creativity.
Iterative Refinement
Get output, then ask: "Make this section shorter," "Add more humor," "This part is confusing." AI improves with direction.
Quick Reference Card
✓ Top 10 Prompt Writing Tips
  • Be specific. Generic prompts get generic results. Add details, constraints, examples.
  • Define the role. "You are a [TYPE] with [EXPERIENCE]" sets tone and expertise.
  • Give context. Explain your situation, constraints, goals, target audience, current state.
  • Specify format. "Format as [BULLETS/TABLE/JSON]" saves time and improves usability.
  • Use brackets. [VARIABLE] notation makes prompts reusable and flexible.
  • Show examples. Few-shot learning (2-3 examples) beats description.
  • Ask for reasoning. "Walk me through your thinking" improves accuracy.
  • Iterate & refine. First output is rarely final. Use follow-ups to improve.
  • One task per prompt. Multiple questions dilute focus. Ask separately for best results.
  • Use constraints creatively. Word limits, tone requirements, and style guides improve output.
